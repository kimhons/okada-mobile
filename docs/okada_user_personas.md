**Document Version**: 1.0  
**Last Updated**: November 2025

## Executive Summary

This document provides detailed user personas for all stakeholders in the Okada quick commerce ecosystem in Cameroon. Understanding these personas is critical for designing user experiences that resonate with the target audience and address their specific needs, challenges, and motivations. The personas are based on research into the Cameroonian market, insights from similar platforms in India (particularly Zepto), and considerations for users with varying levels of digital literacy.

## Customer Personas

### Persona 1: Marie Nkeng - The Busy Professional

**Demographics**
- **Age**: 32 years old
- **Location**: Douala, Bonapriso neighborhood
- **Occupation**: Marketing Manager at a telecommunications company
- **Income**: 450,000 CFA/month (~$750 USD)
- **Education**: University degree in Business Administration
- **Family Status**: Married with one child (4 years old)
- **Language**: Fluent in French and English

**Technology Profile**
- **Smartphone**: Samsung Galaxy A54 (mid-range Android)
- **Internet**: 4G mobile data (MTN Cameroon)
- **Digital Literacy**: High - comfortable with apps and online services
- **Payment Preference**: Mobile Money (MTN MoMo) and bank cards
- **App Usage**: Frequent user of social media, messaging apps, and occasional e-commerce

**Daily Routine**
Marie wakes up at 6:00 AM, prepares her daughter for daycare, and commutes to work by 7:30 AM. She works from 8:00 AM to 5:30 PM, often with meetings extending into the evening. After picking up her daughter from daycare, she arrives home around 7:00 PM, exhausted and with little time for grocery shopping. She values convenience and is willing to pay a premium for services that save her time.

**Goals and Motivations**
- Save time on grocery shopping to spend more quality time with family
- Access fresh, quality products without visiting crowded markets
- Maintain a well-stocked household despite a busy schedule
- Discover new products and recipes for her family
- Manage household expenses efficiently

**Pain Points and Challenges**
- Limited time for grocery shopping due to work commitments
- Traffic congestion makes trips to supermarkets time-consuming
- Traditional markets close early, conflicting with her work schedule
- Carrying heavy groceries while managing her child is difficult
- Inconsistent product availability at local shops
- Concerns about product quality and freshness

**Okada Usage Patterns**
- Orders 3-4 times per week, typically in the evening (7:00-9:00 PM)
- Average order value: 15,000-25,000 CFA (~$25-40 USD)
- Primary categories: Fresh produce, dairy, household essentials, snacks for her daughter
- Prefers scheduled delivery for weekends when planning larger meals
- Uses the app during lunch breaks to browse and add items to cart
- Values real-time order tracking and delivery notifications

**Key Features for Marie**
- Quick reordering of frequently purchased items
- Personalized product recommendations based on purchase history
- Scheduled delivery options for weekend meal planning
- Quality guarantee and easy returns for fresh produce
- Recipe suggestions with one-click ingredient ordering
- Subscription service for regular household items

**Quote**: *"I don't have time to spend hours at the market after work. With Okada, I can order fresh vegetables while I'm in a meeting and have them delivered by the time I get home. It's like having a personal shopper."*

### Persona 3: Maman Josephine - The Traditional Market Shopper

**Demographics**
- **Age**: 54 years old
- **Location**: Douala, New Bell neighborhood
- **Occupation**: Small business owner (fabric shop)
- **Income**: 200,000 CFA/month (~$330 USD)
- **Education**: Secondary school
- **Family Status**: Married with four adult children (two still living at home)
- **Language**: Fluent in French, Duala, and basic English

**Technology Profile**
- **Smartphone**: Samsung Galaxy A03 (entry-level Android)
- **Internet**: Mobile data (MTN), primarily for WhatsApp and calls
- **Digital Literacy**: Low to moderate - comfortable with WhatsApp, struggles with complex apps
- **Payment Preference**: Cash and Mobile Money (MTN MoMo for bill payments)
- **App Usage**: Primarily WhatsApp for business and family communication, occasional Facebook

**Daily Routine**
Maman Josephine opens her fabric shop at 8:00 AM and works until 6:00 PM, with a break at midday to prepare lunch for her family. She traditionally shops at the local market twice a week (Wednesdays and Saturdays) in the early morning before opening her shop. She's accustomed to negotiating prices, inspecting produce carefully, and building relationships with her regular vendors. She's skeptical of new technologies but willing to try them if they're recommended by trusted family members.

**Goals and Motivations**
- Maintain her family's traditional meals and food quality standards
- Save time on market trips to focus more on her business
- Avoid the physical strain of carrying heavy groceries

- Market shopping is physically exhausting, especially in hot weather
- Difficulty reading small text on smartphone screens
- Concerns about product quality when not personally selecting items

- Preference for cash transactions and face-to-face interactions
- Limited understanding of app navigation and features
- Worries about being overcharged or receiving poor-quality products

**Okada Usage Patterns**
- Initially introduced to Okada by her daughter (who resembles Marie persona)
- Orders 1-2 times per week, typically for heavy or bulky items
- Average order value: 20,000-30,000 CFA (~$33-50 USD)
- Primary categories: Rice, cooking oil, canned goods, household cleaning supplies

**Key Features for Maman Josephine**
- Voice-assisted ordering and navigation
- Large, clear buttons and text
- Cash on delivery payment option
- Photo-based product selection (minimal text)
- Customer service call button prominently displayed
- Quality guarantee with easy returns
- Tutorial videos in French explaining how to use the app
- Ability to save favorite orders for easy reordering

**Quote**: *"At first, I didn't trust ordering food I couldn't see and touch. But my daughter showed me how to use Okada, and now I don't have to carry heavy bags of rice from the market. The delivery man is always polite, and I can pay cash when he arrives."*

## Persona Usage Guidelines

### Design Implications

When designing features for the Okada platform, always consider:

1. **Accessibility**: Maman Josephine represents users with low digital literacy. Features must be intuitive with visual cues, voice assistance, and minimal text.

2. **Efficiency**: Marie and Amadou need quick, streamlined experiences. Reduce clicks, enable saved preferences, and provide smart defaults.

3. **Affordability**: Jean-Paul's budget constraints require transparent pricing, promotional opportunities, and value-focused features.

4. **Safety**: Grace's safety concerns highlight the need for robust safety features, especially for female riders working in the evening.

5. **Performance**: Pierre's operational challenges require real-time data, predictive analytics, and efficient workflows.

### Development Priorities

Based on these personas, development should prioritize:

1. **Offline Functionality**: Emmanuel and areas with intermittent connectivity require robust offline capabilities.

2. **Multi-Language Support**: All personas benefit from French/English support, with voice options for lower literacy users.

3. **Payment Flexibility**: Support for Mobile Money (MTN, Orange), cash on delivery, and bank cards to accommodate all users.

4. **Visual Design**: Large, clear buttons and text for users like Maman Josephine, with culturally relevant imagery.

5. **Performance Optimization**: Fast load times and minimal data usage for budget-conscious users like Jean-Paul.

### Testing and Validation

When testing new features:

1. **Include diverse testers** representing each persona type
2. **Test in real-world conditions** (varying connectivity, different times of day)
3. **Observe actual usage patterns** rather than relying solely on surveys
4. **Iterate based on feedback** from users at different digital literacy levels
5. **Validate cultural appropriateness** of language, imagery, and interactions
