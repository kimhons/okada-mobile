# ZeptoNow's Supply Chain and Delivery Model: A Detailed Analysis

## Executive Summary

ZeptoNow's supply chain and delivery model represents a revolutionary approach to quick commerce, built around four core pillars: hyperlocal dark store networks, advanced technology and data analytics, vertically integrated logistics, and diversified monetization strategies. This analysis provides a comprehensive breakdown of how Zepto achieves its 10-minute delivery promise through sophisticated supply chain orchestration.

## 1. Dark Store Network: The Physical Backbone

### Dark Store Concept and Definition

A **dark store** is a micro-fulfillment center that operates exclusively for online order fulfillment, with no public access for in-store shopping. These facilities are strategically positioned in dense residential neighborhoods to minimize last-mile delivery distances and enable ultra-fast order processing.

### Key Characteristics of Zepto's Dark Stores

**Physical Specifications:**
- Size: Compact facilities typically 100-200 square meters
- Setup Cost: ₹25-40 lakh per store
- Coverage Radius: 2-3 km per dark store
- Current Network: 650+ active dark stores (target 1,200 by March 2025)
- Geographic Coverage: 7 major metropolitan cities

**Strategic Location Selection:**
- AI-powered location scouting using data heatmaps
- Analysis of population density, traffic patterns, and purchasing power
- Positioning within high-density residential clusters
- Proximity to target customer demographics

**Store Layout and Design:**
- Optimized for speed rather than customer browsing
- Data-driven product placement with high-velocity items in easily accessible zones
- Utilitarian design focused on storage density and picking efficiency
- System-generated picking paths to minimize collection time
- Limited SKU count (2,500-3,000 high-demand items) for faster processing

### Store-Level Economics

**Profitability Metrics:**
- 50-60% of dark stores are already profitable at store level
- Average setup cost recovery within 12-18 months for mature locations
- High inventory turnover due to focused product selection
- Operational efficiency gains through optimized layouts

- Proprietary route optimization algorithms

**Delivery Economics:**
- Low delivery cost of ₹35-₹45 per order
- Average delivery time: 8 minutes 47 seconds
- Delivery radius: 2-3 km from each dark store
- Real-time tracking and customer communication

### Delivery Process Workflow

**Order Processing:**
1. **Order Placement**: Customer places order via mobile app
2. **Order Routing**: System automatically routes to nearest dark store
3. **Inventory Check**: Real-time availability verification
4. **Picking Assignment**: Optimized picking path generated for store associate
5. **Order Assembly**: Products collected and packed within 60 seconds
6. **Delivery Assignment**: Order assigned to nearest available delivery partner
7. **Route Optimization**: AI-calculated fastest delivery route
8. **Real-time Tracking**: Customer receives live delivery updates
9. **Delivery Completion**: Proof of delivery and customer feedback

**Quality Control Measures:**
- Product quality checks at dark store level
- Temperature-controlled storage for perishables
- Packaging standards for product protection
- Customer feedback integration for continuous improvement

## 9. Supplier Relationships and Procurement Model

### Inventory-Led Business Model

Zepto operates an **"inventory-led"** business model, meaning it owns and manages the products it sells rather than acting as a marketplace or middleman. This approach provides several strategic advantages in supply chain control and customer experience delivery.

**Direct Ownership Benefits:**
- Complete control over product quality and freshness
- Ability to negotiate bulk pricing directly with manufacturers
- Consistent stock levels and availability management
- Reduced dependency on third-party sellers
- Enhanced customer experience through quality assurance

### Supplier Partnership Structure

**Direct Sourcing Strategy:**
- Direct partnerships with FMCG manufacturers and brands
- Elimination of intermediary distributors for better margins
